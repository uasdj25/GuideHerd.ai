# Data Privacy and Responsible Use

**Module:** 1 — AI Foundations  
**Lesson:** 4 of 5  
**Reading time:** ~20 min

---

## Why this matters more for professional services

Professional services firms handle sensitive information as a matter of course: client identities, legal strategy, financial details, health information, personnel records. Most consumer-facing AI tools were not designed with professional confidentiality obligations in mind.

The approved tools in your GuideHerd workflow have been selected with these concerns in mind — but the rules in this lesson apply regardless of which tool you are using.

---

## Rule 1: Use only approved tools

Your firm has approved specific AI tools for specific workflows. Use those tools. Do not use consumer AI tools for firm work involving client information, even for quick tasks.

> **If you are unsure whether a tool is approved:** ask before using it.

---

## Rule 2: Never paste secrets, credentials, or tokens

Do not paste the following into any AI tool under any circumstances:

- Passwords
- API keys or access tokens
- Database connection strings
- Private keys or certificates
- Two-factor authentication codes
- Login credentials of any kind

This applies even if you trust the tool and even if you are just testing something.

---

## Rule 3: Avoid sensitive client data unless the workflow is approved

Sensitive client data includes names combined with identifying details, health information, personnel records, and anything covered by a confidentiality agreement or professional duty.

Before pasting client information into an AI tool, confirm that the workflow has been approved for that type of data.

> **Practical test:** Would you be comfortable if your client saw exactly what you typed into the AI tool? If not, redact it or use a different approach.

---

## Rule 4: Redact unnecessary details

Most AI tasks do not require full identifying information. Before submitting a prompt with client details:

- Replace client names with descriptors ("the client," "the counterparty")
- Remove matter numbers unless they are needed for the task
- Remove specific dates that identify the matter when general timing is sufficient
- Remove financial figures when the task is about structure, not specific amounts

---

## Rule 5: Treat AI output as draft material

AI output should not be stored in a client file, sent to a client, or treated as work product until a qualified person has reviewed and approved it. Keep AI output clearly labeled as draft until reviewed.

---

## Privacy checklist

- [ ] I only use AI tools that have been approved for the type of work I am doing.
- [ ] I never paste passwords, API keys, tokens, or other credentials into any AI tool.
- [ ] I confirm that a workflow is approved for the type of data involved before submitting client information.
- [ ] I redact client names and identifying details when they are not necessary for the task.
- [ ] I treat all AI output as draft material until it has been reviewed by a qualified person.
