# Human Review and When It Is Required

**Module:** 1 — AI Foundations  
**Lesson:** 3 of 5  
**Reading time:** ~20 min

---

## The model is simple

AI prepares. People approve.

This is not a policy suggestion — it is a description of how every GuideHerd workflow is designed. The AI handles drafting, summarizing, organizing, and first-pass analysis. A qualified person reviews the output and decides what to do with it. Nothing goes to a client, into a file, or out the door without that review step.

---

## What human review means in practice

Human review is reading the output with enough attention to catch errors, wrong assumptions, missing information, and scope drift.

For most AI-assisted workflows, review means checking that:

- The output addresses what was actually asked
- No specific facts, figures, or citations appear that you haven't verified
- The content is appropriate for the intended recipient or use
- Nothing has been added beyond the scope of the workflow
- Your professional judgment, not the AI's output, is driving the conclusion

> **The review step is not optional overhead.** It is the point of the workflow. The AI makes the review faster. It does not replace it.

---

## Client-facing drafts always require review

Any output that will be sent to a client, shared externally, or placed in a client file requires review by a qualified person. This includes:

- Emails and letters drafted with AI assistance
- Summaries of client matters or intake information
- Acknowledgments, notices, or follow-up communications
- Anything presented as the firm's work product

The firm owns the output from the moment a qualified person approves it.

---

## High-risk categories always require review

- **Conflict checks.** AI can help organize intake information and flag potential matches. A qualified person makes the conflict determination.
- **Legal filings and formal documents.** Any document to be filed, submitted, or executed requires review by the responsible professional.
- **Privilege and confidentiality assessments.** AI tools do not make these determinations.
- **Medical, financial, or regulated professional advice.** AI assists with workflow steps. It does not provide the regulated professional judgment.
- **Client communication on substantive matters.** Requires review before sending.

---

## Legal and professional services guardrails

GuideHerd training and workflows are operational and administrative:

- GuideHerd does not provide legal advice
- GuideHerd does not make conflict decisions
- GuideHerd does not create attorney-client relationships
- GuideHerd workflows do not send client communications without human approval
- Training focuses on safe use, human review, confidentiality awareness, and workflow consistency

When in doubt about whether something needs review, the answer is yes.
