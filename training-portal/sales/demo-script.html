AI-Assisted Intake Workflow
for a Small Law Firm

Five-minute live demo script. Fictional scenario throughout. All names, firms, and matters are invented. This workflow does not provide legal advice and does not create an attorney-client relationship.

Total time: ~5 minutes
Format: Screen share or live walkthrough
Audience: Firm owner or intake staff

Before you start

  • Have the fictional intake email open and ready to paste
  • Have the AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, or firm-configured tool) open in another tab
  • Have the intake summary prompt ready to paste — do not type live
  • Remind the audience at the start and close that everything is fictional
  • Never use real client names, real matter details, or real case numbers in a live demo

0:00 – 0:30

Opening

Set the scene

Say

"What I'm going to show you takes about five minutes. It's a fictional intake scenario — I'll be playing the role of a prospect emailing a law firm for the first time. Everything you see is made up for the demo. The point is to show you how the workflow handles a real intake situation: disorganized, missing information, some urgency, the kind of thing that hits your inbox every week."

Pause. Confirm they're ready to follow along. If screen-sharing, share your full screen now.

0:30 – 1:30

The intake

Show a realistic, messy intake email

Say

"Here's what came in this morning. This is not unusual — this is what intake actually looks like."
Fictional intake email — paste or display From: Marcus Webb <mwebb_personal@email.com> Subject: need help asap Hi — someone gave me your number but I'm not sure if you handle this kind of thing. My company Hargrove Distribution had a contract with a vendor, Meridian Logistics, and they stopped delivering in February without notice. We had to find someone else last minute and it cost us a lot. My business partner thinks we have a case but I'm not sure. We're also worried about some back and forth emails that might be a problem. Also there's another company that I think might also be suing Meridian so I don't know if that matters. Can someone call me? I'm usually free in the mornings. My number is 555-0182. I think there's some kind of deadline but I don't know what it is. Thanks Marcus

Say

"There are at least five things a staff member needs to pull out of that email before they can do anything with it. Name, company, matter type, urgency signals, potential conflict issues. This is the part that takes time — not because it's hard, but because it has to be done carefully every single time."

1:30 – 2:15

Intake summary

Run the intake summarizer prompt

Say

"I'm going to paste that email into the intake summarizer. This is a prompt we've configured specifically for your firm's intake format. It takes about ten seconds."

Paste the intake email into the AI tool using the firm's intake summarization prompt. Wait for output. Do not narrate while it runs.

Say — after output appears

"Here's what the AI produced. This is a draft — staff still reviews this before doing anything with it. But notice what it did: it pulled the key facts into a consistent format, the same format every time regardless of how messy the original email was."
Expected AI output (approximate) INTAKE SUMMARY — DRAFT FOR STAFF REVIEW Generated: [date] | Source: email | Status: pending review Prospect name: Marcus Webb Company: Hargrove Distribution Contact: 555-0182 (morning availability) Email: mwebb_personal@email.com Matter type: Commercial contract dispute Summary: Prospect's company had a contract with Meridian Logistics. Vendor allegedly stopped delivering in February without notice. Prospect incurred additional costs sourcing alternative. Prospect references emails that may be relevant. Mentions another party potentially in litigation with same vendor. Urgency signal: Prospect references an unknown deadline. Nature and date not specified. Conflict flag: Meridian Logistics — check for existing or prior representation. Second party flag: Unknown third company also referenced in potential Meridian dispute. DRAFT ONLY. Do not act on this summary without staff review and conflict screening.

2:15 – 2:45

Missing information

Show the missing-info flag output

Say

"The intake prompt also runs a missing-information check. Instead of staff having to remember what to ask for, the AI flags what's absent."
Missing information flags — draft for staff review MISSING INFORMATION FLAGS □ Contract dates — when did the contract begin, and what was the agreed delivery period? □ Dollar amount — what were the additional costs incurred? □ Deadline specifics — prospect references a deadline but does not name it. Clarify: statute of limitations, contractual notice period, or other? □ Third-party identity — name of the other company reportedly in dispute with Meridian? □ Relevant emails — prospect mentions emails "that might be a problem." Clarify scope. Recommended: request this information in the first reply, after conflict screening clears.

Say

"Your staff member reviews this, edits anything that doesn't apply, and uses it to draft the follow-up. The AI doesn't send anything — it hands the checklist to a person."

2:45 – 3:15

Urgency indicators

Show urgency flagging

Say

"One more thing the workflow flags: urgency. In this intake, Marcus says 'I think there's some kind of deadline.' That's a signal. A busy intake coordinator might process ten of these in a morning and miss that line. The AI doesn't miss it."
Urgency flags — draft for staff review URGENCY FLAGS ⚠ Prospect mentions an unspecified deadline — nature unknown. Treat as time-sensitive until clarified. ⚠ Alleged breach occurred in February — elapsed time may be relevant to statute of limitations depending on jurisdiction and claim type. IMPORTANT: This workflow does not evaluate legal deadlines or determine statute of limitations. These flags are for staff attention only. A licensed attorney must review all deadline questions.

Say

"Notice the disclaimer at the bottom. The workflow does not interpret legal deadlines. It flags the signal and hands it to someone who can."

3:15 – 3:45

Conflict-screening prep

Show the conflict-screening checklist

Say

"The workflow also prepares a conflict-screening checklist. This is not a conflict check — your staff does that. The AI pulls the names from the intake and formats them so the person doing the conflict check doesn't have to."
Conflict-screening prep — complete before proceeding CONFLICT-SCREENING CHECKLIST Names to screen (from intake): □ Marcus Webb (prospect / individual) □ Hargrove Distribution (prospect / company) □ Meridian Logistics (adverse party) □ Unknown third company (referenced — name to be confirmed) Staff action required: □ Run all names through firm conflict database □ Check for prior representation of Meridian Logistics or related parties □ Confirm third-party identity before screening is complete □ Document screening result before any substantive communication IMPORTANT: This checklist does not perform a conflict check. The conflict-screening decision is made by firm staff, not by this workflow.

Say

"The AI prepared the checklist. A person completes it and signs off. That's the workflow."

3:45 – 4:30

Draft acknowledgment

Show the draft acknowledgment

Say

"After conflict screening clears, the workflow can generate a draft acknowledgment. This is the first reply to the prospect. The AI drafts it — staff reads it, edits it if needed, and approves it before it goes anywhere."
Draft acknowledgment — for staff review and approval before sending Subject: Re: need help asap Dear Mr. Webb, Thank you for reaching out. We received your message and will be in touch shortly to discuss the situation you described involving Hargrove Distribution and Meridian Logistics. Before we can speak in detail, we need to complete a brief internal review. We will contact you to schedule a time to talk, typically within 1–2 business days. If your situation is time-sensitive, please let us know by replying to this email. [Staff name] [Firm name] --- DRAFT ONLY — review before sending. This message does not constitute legal advice and does not create an attorney-client relationship. Staff: remove this line before sending.

Say

"The disclaimer at the bottom is for your staff — it reminds them this is a draft. They remove it before sending. The AI does not send this. Your staff member reads it, adjusts the tone if needed, fills in the bracketed fields, and hits send."

4:30 – 5:00

Close

Wrap up and invite questions

Say

"That's the full workflow — intake in, structured summary out, flags raised, conflict checklist prepared, draft acknowledgment ready for review. Five steps. Everything reviewed by a person before anything happens. The AI did the organizing. Your staff did the deciding."

Say

"To be clear about what this is and isn't: everything I just showed you was a fictional scenario. Marcus Webb, Hargrove Distribution, Meridian Logistics — all invented. And the workflow itself does not provide legal advice, does not make conflict decisions, and does not contact anyone without your approval. It is a tool for your staff, not a replacement for them."

Say

"Questions?"

Common follow-up topics: what AI tool it uses, how intake arrives (email vs. form), whether it connects to their practice management system, what the setup looks like, and pricing. See the offer sheet for setup details.
