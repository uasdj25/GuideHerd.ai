AI-Assisted Intake Workflow

A plain-English explainer for small professional firms considering AI-assisted intake.

§ 01

What it does

When a new inquiry arrives — by email, web form, or voicemail transcript — an AI-assisted intake workflow reads it and prepares a structured summary for staff review. It does not replace staff. It gives them a better starting point.

  • Organizes unstructured intake messages into a readable summary
  • Flags likely missing information (contact details, matter type, dates)
  • Identifies urgency signals (deadlines, emergency language, statute of limitations references)
  • Prepares a conflict-screening checklist for staff to complete
  • Drafts an initial acknowledgment for human review and approval before sending
§ 02

What it does not do

These limits are not caveats. They are design decisions. The workflow is built around a single rule: AI prepares, people approve.

  • Does not provide legal advice, opinions, or professional guidance
  • Does not make conflict-screening decisions — it prepares a checklist for staff
  • Does not create an attorney-client or professional relationship
  • Does not send any communication to a client or prospect without human review and approval
  • Does not take any action on its own — all outputs are drafts or review materials
  • Does not replace a paralegal, intake coordinator, or attorney review
§ 03

Why it helps

Speed

Staff get a structured summary in seconds instead of reading and re-reading a disorganized email to extract the key facts.

Consistency

Every intake goes through the same checklist. Missing information and urgency signals get caught regardless of how busy the day is.

Reduced follow-up

When the AI flags missing information early, staff can request it in the first reply rather than in the third or fourth.

Better handoffs

A structured intake summary makes it easier for one staff member to hand an inquiry to another without losing context.

§ 04

Best fit

This workflow works best for firms with the following characteristics:

Firm size

1–15 staff. Large enough to have intake volume that benefits from structure. Small enough that one inefficient intake step affects the whole day.

Intake channel

Email, web form, or any source that produces a text-based inquiry. Voicemail transcripts also work if already converted to text.

Industry

Law firms, accounting practices, financial advisors, and other professional services firms where intake involves collecting facts, not just scheduling.

Review culture

Firms where staff are comfortable reviewing AI output and approving or editing before acting. Not suitable where all output is expected to be automatic.

§ 05

Typical setup

Most engagements move through three stages over two to four weeks.

Week 1–2

Intake audit and workflow design

Review current intake process, identify what information the firm actually needs at intake, define the conflict-screening checklist, and document the approval step. No AI tools are configured until this is complete.

Week 2–3

Prompt and template build

Configure intake summarization, missing-info detection, urgency flagging, and draft acknowledgment prompts specific to the firm's matter types and communication style. Test against real (anonymized) intake examples.

Week 3–4

Staff training and handoff

Train the staff who will use the workflow. Cover how to read AI summaries, how to use the checklists, and how the approval step works. Deliver written SOPs. Optional: ongoing monthly support after handoff.
