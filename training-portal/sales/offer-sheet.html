§ 02

What's included in setup

Setup is a one-time engagement. Everything produced is delivered to you and documented for your staff to use independently.

Intake audit

Review of your current intake process: how inquiries arrive, what information you need at intake, where the handoffs happen, and what delays or errors occur most often.

Prompt library

Four to six configured prompts specific to your firm:

  • Intake summarizer
  • Missing-information detector
  • Urgency flagging
  • Conflict-screening prep
  • Draft acknowledgment
  • Guardrail check (for staff use)

Conflict-screening checklist

A structured checklist template for your staff to complete after each intake. Designed around your matter types and practice areas.

Staff SOP

A written standard operating procedure covering how to run the workflow, how to review AI output, how to complete the conflict-screening checklist, and how the approval step works.

Staff training session

One 60–90 minute live session with the staff who will use the workflow. Covers the full workflow end-to-end, the human review model, and what to do when AI output looks wrong.

Testing against real intakes

Before handoff, prompts are tested against anonymized examples from your actual intake — not generic scenarios. Output is reviewed and prompts are adjusted until results are consistent.

§ 03

What's included in monthly support

Optional after setup. Most firms start with three to six months of support while the workflow becomes routine, then continue or cancel based on need.

  • One 30-minute monthly check-in call — review how the workflow is performing, flag issues
  • Prompt updates when AI model behavior changes or firm intake patterns shift
  • Minor workflow adjustments — new matter type, revised acknowledgment language, updated checklist items
  • Email support for staff questions between check-ins
  • Annual review of SOPs and checklists to confirm they reflect current firm practice

Not included in support: Major workflow redesign, new practice area buildout, or additional staff training sessions. These are scoped separately if needed.

§ 04

What this workflow does not do

These are not limitations to work around. They are the design. Every output the workflow produces is a draft or checklist for human review — not a decision, not a final product, not a communication.

  • Does not provide legal advice, professional opinions, or compliance guidance
  • Does not make conflict-screening decisions — prepares checklists for staff to complete
  • Does not create an attorney-client relationship
  • Does not send any communication to a client or prospect without human review and approval
  • Does not connect to or modify your practice management system
  • Does not operate automatically — a person runs each workflow step
  • Does not retain or store client data beyond the session it is used in
