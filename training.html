The problem, named.

Three ways a system becomes shelfware.

1

The capable tool, the helpless team.

The system ships. Nobody owns it. The consultant leaves. Twelve months later the workflow is manual again.

2

The training that doesn't transfer.

One session, one slide deck, one certificate nobody can find. Staff nods along and returns to what they know.

3

The knowledge cliff.

AI models, tools, and libraries change quarterly. Teams trained once fall behind fast. The system becomes a liability instead of an asset.

What the training covers.

Three pillars. One curriculum.

A

AI Foundations

"Understanding what you're working with."

Before anyone can use the system well, they need a working mental model of what AI agents actually do — and don't do. This track removes the mysticism and replaces it with practical intuition.

  • How large language models work (no math required)
  • Prompt design and iteration basics
  • What AI is unreliable at — and why that matters for your workflow
  • Data privacy, compliance exposure, and responsible use
  • AI governance basics for professional services environments
B

Platform Proficiency

"Running the system we built for you."

Every GuideHerd deployment is customized to a client's practice. This track covers the specific agents, dashboards, and automations running in the client's environment — and trains staff to operate, troubleshoot, and hand off tasks without calling us.

  • Matter intake and conflict-check agents (for legal clients)
  • Document review and drafting assistants
  • Time capture and billing automation
  • Dashboard navigation and report interpretation
  • Escalation protocols and edge-case handling
C

Ongoing Skills Maintenance

"Staying current without becoming a full-time learner."

The AI landscape shifts quarterly. New models drop. Tools change. Libraries deprecate. This track is the recurring cadence that keeps your team's skills current — without requiring them to live in the AI press.

  • Monthly model and tool update briefings (curated, not firehose)
  • Quarterly capability refreshes tied to GuideHerd platform updates
  • Emerging use cases relevant to your practice area
  • Internal champion updates and train-the-trainer sessions

Certification program.

Certification, not just completion.

Completing a track earns a GuideHerd certification. This is not a participation trophy — it documents that the person demonstrated competency in a specific set of tasks against a real workflow.

What certification includes

  • 01 Written assessment — open-book, scenario-based, graded against documented criteria
  • 02 Live task demonstration — the candidate runs a real workflow inside the platform, observed by a GuideHerd trainer
  • 03 SOP sign-off — the candidate's documented procedures reviewed and approved before certification is issued
  • 04 Digital credential — a verifiable record issued to the individual and the firm

Why it matters

  • — Creates internal accountability — certified staff have a documented standard to maintain, not just a session they attended
  • — Signals compliance-readiness to partners — relevant for firms under regulatory scrutiny or with client-facing AI workflows
  • — Gives staff a documented professional development record — something portable and real, not just another vendor completion badge
On maintenance: Certifications are maintained through the ongoing skills track. A certification that goes unmaintained for 12 months lapses.
